Bugatti

Bugatti Chiron

All Chiron models are motivated by an 8.0-liter 16-cylinder powerplant. This beast of an engine employs four turbochargers to generate a mighty 1500 horsepower and 1180 pound-feet of torque. Bugatti claims that the Chiron makes the dash from zero to 60 mph in a mere 2.3 seconds, and it has a top speed of 261 mph.

Bugatti Veyron

The heart and soul of the Veyron is the 8.0-liter W16 engine with four turbochargers, which initially generated 1,001 PS, and an incredible 1,200 PS in later models. The 16-cylinder mid-engine is 710 mm in length, no longer than a conventional V12 engine, and weighs just 490 kg due to its lightweight construction.

Divo

The Divo is not only lighter but more aerodynamic and has better handling than the Chiron. It generates 198 pounds more downforce than the Chiron, aided by the six-foot-wide hydraulic wing that is 25% wider than the wing on the Chiron.
